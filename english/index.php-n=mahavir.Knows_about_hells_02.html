How to protect from hells

We have now come to know that men who perform sinful activities and are aggressive against others including animals, birds and insects get punishment in various hells as described above. How to escape from these dreadful hells? Cause of sufferings

Various miseries torture humanity because of three things , 1. Sins (papa), 2. The seed of sin (papa-bij), and 3. Nescience (avidya)

Sins that we have committed are twofold ; (1) Prarabdha and (2) Aprarabdha. When the fruits of sins have matured they are called Prarabdha.; When the fruits of sins are yet to mature (in future) they are known as Aprarabdha.

A person who is fortunate enough to obtain Bhakti is always freed from sinful activities. Even when he attains strong faith which is qualification for gaining Bhakti he abstains from the sinful actions. Sinful impulses, the seed of sin also finds no place in the heart of those who are sanctified by pure devotion. Error regarding the knowledge of one‘s self (Jivatma) is nescience. While making progress towards unalloyed devotion, a jiva is enabled to understand his true nature. It experiences that it is not an entity of the material world nor has it any permanent relationship with its physical and mental cases in which it is prisoner at present. It comes to know that it is an eternal servant of the Supreme Lord. The cause of all our sufferings is due to misidentification, resulting from our averseness to Godhead.

When the light of unalloyed devotion illuminates the dark chambers of our hearts, the root cause of all such sufferings is completely eradicated leaving no room for any further miseries.

In Srimad Bhagavatam ( 11-14-18), Lord Krishna said :-

“Just as a blazing fire reduces every piece of fuel to ashes even so unflinching devotion to Me (God), strikes at the root of all sins.” For those seeking liberation there is nothing more efficacious in cutting the root of sins than chanting the Name and glories of Lord Vishnu.

Utterance of name of Sri Krishna destroys all sins. (ch.ch.adi. 8/24)

A single utterance of the Name of Krishna destroys all sins. If one utters Sri Krishna nama he gets loving devotion.

Sri Hari –nama, Hari –Nama and Hari-Nama alone is the Sole Resort ; there is no other , no other , no other way except Sri Hari –Nama in the age of Kali Yuga. In Kali Yuga the primeval Lord prevails verily in the Form of Sri Hari-Nama. Sri Hari Nama is Hari Himself. It is pulsating with kindness. If a person, on seeing devotees engrossed in the chanting of the Name, becomes attached to them, he achieves Goloka of Sri Krishna and rejoices in His company. Sri Krishna says, O Partha ! Take the Names with a firm determination, as a person who is saturated with the Name is dear to Me ; O Arjuna! I again implore you to take to the service of the Name.

Ajamil- the resort of all sins, uttering `Narayana` under the semblance of calling his younger son, went to Vaikuntha. How much better would the result be , if it is uttered with implicit faith?

No sinner is capable of committing sins to the extent that a single utterance of the Name of the Lord could destroy. The capacity of the Name to destroy the sins is far greater that the sinners` capacity to commit sins.

But we should keep in our mind that the seed of Sri Krishna Nama never sprouts in the heart of those who commits offences against the Name. According to Padma Purana there are ten offences. These offences will postpone the realization of the fruit of Sri Nama- Sankirtan which is Krishna Prem. Aspirants indulging in such offences will not taste the incomparable Bliss flowing from the Eternal Fount of Sri Krishna Nama. These offences are described below :-

First offence - Speaking ill of Sadhus, publishing false scandals about them, causing harm or injury to their persons or adopting other violent methods , like disrespecting them, speaking harsh and impolite words of them. These activities form an offence against the Name. Chanter of divine Name should avoid all such activities to get fruit of Hari Nama. Lord Hari punish those who act against His devotees.

Second Offence :- To regard Sri Vishnu as absolutely different from His Names , Form, and Qualities etc., form an offence. To treat Lord Vishnu (Krishna) equal to demigods like Shiva etc., or treat Shiva etc., independent of Him, is also an offence. Sri Krishna is the Supreme Godhead, the transcendental autocrat. He is the Prime Cause of all other causes, the God of all gods and the Original of all objects and entities. All other gods are powerful owning to the inspiration they receive from Him They are all delegated with certain powers by Him. Just as even a costly and highly powerful electric bulb is of no use without current from the power-house, even so, deprived of Sri Krishna`s energy, all these gods are powerless entities. Sri Krishna is the Transcendental power-house from Whom all these gods receive inspiration. To treat Him as on a par with other gods who are not independent and who receive strength from Him is an offence against the Holy Name. All Demigods are under influence three modes of Maya whereas Lord Krishna is beyond the three modes of Maya.

Third Offence: Disregarding the Spiritual Preceptor (Gurudeva) is third offence. He who chants Hari Nama at all times is the real preceptor. Such a spiritual master has no other function than the chanting of the Name of Sri Hari. He is the greatest exponent of the Holy Name. The Guru though he appears before us, talks to us in our language, dresses like us, takes food like others and behaves with us like a human being, but he is not an ordinary mortal. He is purely a transcendental personage. One should not speak lie or cheat Gurudeva. We should obey commands of our Gurudeva. The advice of the Guru should be obeyed unconditionally without any discrimination. Discarding the Guru and his words of advice, no disciple can realise the Absolute Truth. The Name of the Lord never manifests on the tongue of one who disregards is Sad-Guru. It is a greatest offence. In order to know more about personality of Gurudeva one should study `Gurudeva over tops disciple` written by Tridandi Swami Bhakti Prabhav Mahavir Maharaj.

For advancement in spiritual life, one must strictly follow the bona -fide instructions of the Sri Gurudeva. As confirmed in the Vedic injunctions , yasya deve para bhaktir yatha deve tatha gurau. One should have equal faith in the God and Gurudeva, who helps the disciple make progress in spiritual life. As soon as the disciple thinks independently, not caring for the instructions of the spiritual master, he is a failure as kGKurudeva becomes dissatisfied ( yasya aprasadan na gatih kuto api). Aditi very strictly followed the instructions of her husband and Spiritual Master, and thus she was successful to get Lord Vamandeva as her son.

Fourth Offence :- Criticism of scriptures is an offence against Holy name. The scriptures are not created by any mortal. They originate directly from the Supreme Lord Himself; accordingly, they are understood by the wise as self-revealed truths. Sri Vyasdeva, one of the Manifestations of the Lord expounded and expanded same for the enlightenment of the ignorant persons.

Maya mugdh jever nahi krishna-smriti –gyan
Jevere kripay kaila krishna ved-puran II (ch.ch.m 20/122)

People who are under the influence of Maya (illusion), have no knowledge about Sri Krishna Hence, Sri Krishna has manifested out of His Mercy, the Vedas and Puranas. From the breath of the Supreme Person, have originated the four Vedas, the great epics, all the Puranas, Upnishads, sloka , sutra etc. Therefore scriptures should not be criticized on any ground.

Fifth Offence: To understand that glory of Divine Name is not true, is also an offence against Holy Name. Less intelligent persons are of opinion that the praises on the efficacy of the Holy Name are simply hyperbolic. But it not true. Glory of Divine Name is true and powerful. One should chant Name of God with firm faith. By chanting the Name of Hari even a patient or a drunkard becomes eternally free and attains the pleasure of Sri Maha Vishnu. Holy Name is identical with Hari. Divine Name has all powers of Lord Hari. There is no difference between Hari and His Names.

Sixth Offence: Most people are given to a belief that Godhead has no Name. They are of opinion that any object with a Name or Form is the production of Maya or imaginary. Their opinion on transcendental objects is totally mistaken. This is an offence. God has numberless names and forms. His all Names, Forms, Qualities, Abode etc., are not imaginary but true.

Seventh Offence: Committing repeatedly sins on the strength of Name is also an offence. After listening to the marvellous Glories of the Divine Name if one continue sinful activities thinking that all sins will be destroyed by chanting Names of God. It is offence to commit sin on the dint of Name. The Divine Name by chanting which Divine Love is achieved should never be used for destroying One`s sins. Sri Gauranga Mahaprabhu while at Navadvip converted two rowdies—Jagai and Madhai into great saints. He got an assurance from them to that effect. He said ;-

“You must not commit any more sins”

Eighth offence : Regarding the Holy Name in the same category as that of all other religious practices such as moral duties, religious vows, renunciaton, sacrifice etc., Chanting of Sri Hari nama is direct service of Lord Krishna. Sankirtan should not be compared with other religious practices. It is an offence against Holy Name of God. One utterance of the Name “Krishna” is more beneficial than the performance of even crores of horse –sacrifices. Yamaraj will punish that infidel, who compares a single utterance of ‘Sri Krishna –Name with even crores of horse –sacrifices.

Ninth offence : Imparting the Holy Name to persons who have no faith in and are averse to chanting the Holy Name, is an offence. Those who are irreligious from their very birth, constitutes an offence. The Holy Name should not be imparted to others, out of selfish motive, such as receiving money, earning a name and fame , etc. This would amount to a misuse of the Holy Name. This is a great offence against Holy Name.

Tenth Offence : After listening the excellences of the Name of Lord Hari if one does not have interest for chanting and hearing the same, he commits an offence. There should be no bargaining with the Holy Name for worldly gains. The Name must be uttered for Its own sake. The aspirant should chant the Name in a spirit of self-surrender, invoking at every step the blessings of his Spiritual Master .

These are ten offences against Holy Nama. One should not commit sins as mentioned above. Sri Gauranga Mahaprabhu while in Nabdvipa Dhama, converted two rowdies—Jagai and Madhai into great saints. They committed all types of sins. There were no sins that these two did not commit before their conversion. In a couple of minutes both became ideal devotees. Nine-fold devotion

Bhajner Madhya sresta nava vidha bhakti\\ Krishna prema, Krishna dite dhare maha shakti

When analyse various devotional methods mentioned in our Puranans, nine-fold ways of devotion declared in Srimad Bhagavatam, occupy a very high position. Puranas described sixty-four methods of devotional practices. Nine-fold devotion award us love for Sri Krishna. Here we describe ninefold devotional practices taught by Prahlad to his father Hrinyakasyapu, a demon.

Srvanam kirtanam vishnoh smaranam padsevanam
Archanam vandanam dasyam sakhyam-atmaanivedanam (Bhagvatam. 7-5-23)

1- Sravana ( listening):
Sravana means listening the Names, Divine pastimes, Attributes etc., of the Supreme Lord Sri Krishna. In spiritual life first procedure is to hear Holy Name .On hearing the marvels of the Name, when the mind is purified, the next stage of hearing the topics relating to the Transcendental Form of the Lord is to be followed. If Names, Forms, Qualities, Pastimes and Entourages of the Lord are heard from a genuine Devotion, it is far more effective. Hearing from one who is a disbeliever is not recommended. Here we should keep in my mind that off all kinds of sacred books prescribed for the hearing , the act of listening to Srimad Bhagavata is spoken of as the best. It is composed by the great Krishna Dvapayan Vedavyas, one of the Lord`s Manifestations . It contains the quintessence of all the sastras of the world.

2. Kirtan : Kirtan means chanting aloud the Names, Form, Qualities, Encourage and Lilas of Supreme Lord. After hearing these blissful and auspicious Glories relating to the Divine births and deeds of Sri Krishna manifested in the world, one must chant them honestly. Anyone who has fallen into this stormy ocean of worldliness can get deliverance from it in no time by uttering His Name. Sri Chaitanya said : “Dance and chant in the association of vaishnava devotees the glories of Lord Krishna”. Nacho gao bhakta sange karoh sankirtana

3. Smarana: Remembering the Names, Forms, Qualities and Sportive activities of the Supreme Lord is known as Samarana. . Smarana is five- fold according to the stages of development.

(i) The primary stage of Smarana is recalling to one`s mind the Holy Name, Form, Attributes etc of the Lord.

(ii) Dharana : This is a hard stage. A regular tug -of -war between him on one side and his mind with all its senses on the other side. The mind running mad like a made horse escaping from its keeper is to be restricted from its wanderings. Withdrawing the mind from all its diverse objectives and fixing it in remembering the Lord is the meaning of this state of mental pursuit . It is called Dharana.

(iii) Dhyan : Special concentration of the mind on the Form and activities etc., of the Lord is meant by Dhyana.At this stage striver has succeeded in his attempts to control the mind. He is free from outside distractions and the mad influence of the fickle mind . He can concentrate mind upon the Lord.

(iv) Dhruvanu Smrit: The incessant flow of the mind like a continuous stream of nectar towards the Supreme Lord.

(v) Samadhi : The spontaneous manifestation of the Lord in the heart. This stage of Samadhi is different from the one that is attained by the Gyanis generally known as Brhama-Samadhi. This are five progressive stages of Samarna.

4. Padasevana :: This mode of devotion is the act of seeing, touching, circumambulating the Holy Image and the sacred temple , sacred abodes like Vrindavan Mathura, Jagannatha , Dvarka, Ayodhya,etc.; going on pilgrimages bathing in the holy rivers like the Ganges, Yamuna, etc. Taking shelter in any of the sacred abodes of the Lord or holy temple for whole life., and service of the sacred Tulasi. All these come under the category of Padasevan

5. Archana : Ritualistic worship of the Lord is Archana. Every householder should perform this process. This ultimately leads a devotee to the Lord. Just as watering the root of a tree nourishes its main trunk, branches and twinges, so also the worship of Sri Vishnu results in the satisfaction of all souls including Him. If Sri Krishna is served, all the lesser gods are definitely served. Lord Krishna said in Srimad Bhagavatam (11-27-49), “He who worships Me adopting the regulated processes prescribed by the Vedas and tantra texts, receives from Me rewards in fulfillment of all his desires here and hereafter.”

6. Vandan: Vandan is spoken part of Archana. The sense of extreme humility on hearing the unparalleled Glories of the Lord is the main basis of this method. Vandan means Namaskar. Namaskar indicates the giving up of all types of material ego. Sastanga Namaskar means bowing down to the Lord as a mark of respect and in utter humility,. utilizing all the Indriyas viz,; 1. the arms 2. the legs, 3. the knees, 4. the breast, 5., the head , 6. the eyes, 7. mind , 8. words. One must bow down to the Lord with his heart and soul, with the eyes slightly opened and earnestly feeling and uttering. (Hari Bhakti Vilas 1-8-360). Besides this there is the other method known as the Panchanga Namaskar, which means prostrating oneself before the Lord with 1. the knees, 2. the hands, 3. the head .4. with words, 5. mind dedicated to Him. This is called panchanga dandvat pranama.

7 Dasya: In this method there is sincere feeling that one is the servant of the Lord. A servant of the Lord occupies a position second to none. In all forms of devotion this feeling of rendering service to the Supreme Lord plays the most important role. To attain the position of a servant of Lord needs Herculean efforts.

8. Sakhya : This means friendly relationship form with the Lord. It includes various comforts and timely services of the Lord in a friendly way. Just as a sincere friend in the world, is always eager for the welfare of his companion, so too a genuine devotee of this order, puts all his loving efforts in the interest of the Lord.

9. Atmanivedan: This is complete self-surrender. This consists of surrendering unconditionally one`s body, mind, words, soul and all that one feels as his own to the Lord. A devotee of this order is utterly indifferent to make efforts even for his own purposes but he never abstains from rendering services to the Lord. Even after complete self-surrender, the surrendered soul feels that he is the property of the Lord and has nothing that he can claim as his own. Such a devotee never worries for his daily necessities such as food, clothing, etc. or even medicine during all sorts of illness. Atmanivedan distinguished from Saranagati in respect of certain special characteristics. In a word, Atmanivedan is the fully developed condition of Saranagati.

This is called nine-fold devotion. Amongst the nine-fold ways of devotion, Nama-Sankirtan tops the list. Tar madhye sarv srestha nama- sankirtan. Nir-apradhe nama laile pay prem-dhana . But Nama should be uttered without committing any sins. Nama-Sankirtana should be performed free from all the offences.

The Names of the Supreme Lord Krishna are grouped under two heads :--- 1. Specific, 2. Generic. Specific names are Krishna, Hari, Govinda, Gopal, Nandakishor, Radharamana, Murari, Mukunda, Rama, etc., Sages insists upon the aspirants to call Him by His Specific Names only which attract Him surely and quickly. Generic names are Brahman, Paramatma, Bhagavan, Isvar, Jagadish, Vibhu, Prabhu, Malika, Ananta, Sarvasaktiman, parmesvar etc., These names denote the transcendental power of the Lord. God has no attraction for His Generic Names. Sri Krishna is enamoured and enasnared by His Specific Names only.

Great Shiva said, “One single Name ‘Rama’ is equivalent to the utterance of one thousands other Names of Sri Vishnu. Then again as single utterance of the word ‘Krishna’ once, grandly accomplishes the result for which the sacred thousand names of Sri Vishnu have to be repeated thrice.”

One Rama nama = one thousand names of Vishnu

One Krishna nama = three thousands names of Sri Vishnu.

Utterance of three names of Rama = One name of Sri Krishna (Rama Rama Rama = Krishna)

Supreme Lord Krishna Chaitanya Mahaprabhu gave advice to house-holders :

Prabhu kahen----Krishna-seva, vaishnava –sevan
Nirantar kar Krishna-nama sankirtana

“Serve Sri Krishna and His devotees and constantly perform the sankirtan of the Name of Sri Krishna . This is teaching of Lord Krishna Chaitanya Mahaprabhu for spiritual benefit.. By the single name of Sri Krishna all your sins are destroyed and it helps the appearance of nine -fold devotion. The name is independent and All powerful. Hence , It does not require the assistance of any other process for the fulfilment of Its Efficacies. It does not even wait for initiation or such other ritualistic.

In the present age of Kali Yuga always chant the following Mahamantra and be happy. This Mahamantra is superior to all other mantras. Devotees should chant with firm faith. Best time for chanting is before sunrise. Chanting this mahamantra is protection from hells and gettimg ever existisng bliss.

Hare Krishna Hare Krishna Krishna Krishna Hare Hare
Hare Rama Hare Rama Rama Rama Hare Hare

(Brahmand Purana , Uttar khand 6/56)
